Biblical Valuation System

In ancient Israel, individuals dedicated to the Lord were assigned values in silver shekels based on their age and gender.

  • Males (20-60 years) were valued at 50 shekels.
  • Females (20-60 years) were valued at 30 shekels.
  • Different age groups had different assigned values.

This valuation system was part of Israelite religious customs and was used for offerings made to the sanctuary.

Understanding the Levitical Valuation

The Levitical Valuation is a system from the Bible (Leviticus 27:1-8) where a person could dedicate themselves to God by making a vow. Instead of physically serving in the temple, they could offer a set amount of silver based on their age and gender. This amount was used as a financial contribution to the sanctuary.
